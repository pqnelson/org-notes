1. Examples

1.1. Stacks as Categorified Sheaves

An $n$-stack is an $(n+1)$-sheaf.

If \(\cat{C}\) is an $n$-site, then we could define $n$-Sheaves on \(\cat{C}\) as 2-sheaves taking values in $(n-1)$-categories. (C.f., nLab entry on $n$-localic 2-topos, specifically Grothendieck $n$-toposes; and n-localic 2-topos entry's discussion of n-sheaves.)

We could also consider $(n,1)$-sheafs as higher versions.

1.2. Differential Calculus Categorifies as Goodwillie Calculus

The $(∞,1)$-categorification of differential calculus is the Goodwillie calculus.
