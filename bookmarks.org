- Danil Annenkov, Mikkel Milo, Jakob Botsch Nielsen, Bas Spitters,\\
  "Extracting functional programs from Coq, in Coq".\\
  {{{arXiv(2108.02995)}}}, 57 pages
- Herman Geuvers,\\
  "The Calculus of Constructions and Higher Order Logic".\\
  In /The Curry-Howard isomorphism/ (ed. Ph. de Groote)\\
  [[http://www.cs.ru.nl/~herman/PUBS/CC_CHiso.pdf][Preprint]], discusses inconsistency in extensions of HOL (e.g., HOL +
  (prop = type) is inconsistent).
  - Geuvers actually uses this as grounds for arguing ~prop != type~ in
    general (Automath experiment also shows if ~prop = type~, then the
    law of double negation acts like a Hilbert choice operator, due to
    proof irrelevance being "the same" as type non-emptiness --- another
    reason why ~prop != type~ is a good idea).
- Robert Harper,\\
  "An Equational Logical Framework for Type Theories".\\
  [[https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.01484][arXiv:2106.01484]], 9 pages.

  [[https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.03389][arXiv:2105.03389]], 16 pages.

  "A Framework for Reasoning About LF Specifications".\\

  Also presents a unique variant of Canonical LF in chapter 2.
